## The 5 Questions

### Question 1 — Your role
*What best describes your current role?*
- HR / People operations
- Sales / Business development
- Finance / Accounts
- Operations / Admin
- Marketing
- IT / Technical support
- Other

---

### Question 2 — Your Excel habits
*Which of these have you done in Excel in the last month? (select all that apply)*
- VLOOKUP or XLOOKUP
- PivotTable
- SUMIF or COUNTIF
- Filter + copy-paste to new sheet
- Manual report every week or month
- Merging data from multiple files manually

---

### Question 3 — Your most painful data task
*What is your most painful, repetitive data task right now?*

Be specific. "Every Monday I copy headcount from 3 files into one summary" is perfect.
"I work in sales" is not useful here.

We will try to solve exactly your described task using SQL during the course.

This is on the **Google Form** (not Zoom registration) — there is no character limit.

---

### Question 4 — Your tech comfort level
*How comfortable are you with technology and new tools right now?*
- Not at all comfortable — this is new territory
- Somewhat — I manage but slowly
- Fairly comfortable
- Very comfortable

---

### Question 5 — Your SQL feeling
*Complete this sentence: "When I hear the word SQL, I feel..."*
- Confused — I have no idea what it is
- Nervous — it sounds very technical
- Curious but unsure
- Neutral — just another tool
- Excited — I've been wanting to learn this

---

## Why This Matters

Your answers help Priya:
- Choose which industry examples to use in the live demos
- Know how technical to be in the first hour
- Connect SQL concepts to the actual tasks you do at work
- Start Day 1 with "I know you do X every week — here is how SQL fixes that"

Honest answers are more useful than impressive ones. No one is judging your Excel skills.
