# 3 Videos to Watch Before Day 1

These 3 videos are all you need. Do not watch anything else.
Longer is not better here — 20 minutes of focused video is enough.

---

## Video 1 — What is a database? (6 min)

**Channel:** Decomplexified
**Search YouTube for:** "What is a database Decomplexified"

**Why watch this:**
The clearest non-technical explanation of what a database is and why it exists. No jargon. No coding. Just the concept explained with examples that make sense.

**What to notice:**
The analogy between a spreadsheet and a database table. When you see it, you will understand why we use databases instead of Excel for large-scale data.

---

## Video 2 — SQL in 100 seconds (2 min)

**Channel:** Fireship
**Search YouTube for:** "SQL in 100 seconds Fireship"

**Why watch this:**
The fastest overview of SQL that exists. Dense and useful. Watch it twice — the second time you will catch things you missed.

**What to notice:**
SQL is described as "a language to talk to databases." That is all it is. Not programming. Not math. Talking to a database.

---

## Video 3 — SSMS First Look (12 min)

**Channel:** WiseOwlTutorials
**Search YouTube for:** "SSMS first look WiseOwl"

**Why watch this:**
Shows exactly the interface you will use on Day 1. The Object Explorer, the Query window, how to run a query with F5. Nothing on Day 1 will look unfamiliar if you have watched this.

**What to notice:**
How to open a New Query window and run a basic SELECT statement. Pay attention to where the database dropdown is — you will use it every session.

---

## Total Time: Approximately 20 Minutes

After watching, you do not need to remember anything specific.

The goal is **recognition** — when you see SSMS on Day 1, it should look familiar. When Priya says "SELECT FROM WHERE", you should already have a rough idea of what that means.

---

## A Note on YouTube Rabbit Holes

SQL has thousands of tutorials on YouTube. Most of them are good. Most of them are also unnecessary before Day 1.

If you watch one of these 3 videos and want to explore more — that is fine. But resist the urge to go deeper before the session. You will learn more in 3 hours with a facilitator and a real database than in 3 hours of solo YouTube watching.

The prep is done. Day 1 is where the real work starts.
